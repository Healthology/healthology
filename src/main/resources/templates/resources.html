Seeking Help

+
Mental health involves a level of psychological well-being. Mental health includes emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It can affect how we feel, think, and how we act. Mental health can be associated with our ability able to handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions. It is an important factor in your ability to function everyday. Mental illnesses are typically serious disorders that can negatively affect your thinking, mood, and behavior. Your mental health is important in every stage of your life. People with mental health problems can get better, and many of them recover completely.

  • Staying positive because a positive attitude can go a long way. Take a second to look at one good thing that happened in your day, even if it was something small.

  • Make strong connections with others because your relationships play a major role in your mental health.

  • Develop a new sense of meaning and purpose in life. Find something that you enjoy and do those things. Having a hobby might be beneficial and give you something to look forward to.

  • Getting enough sleep. Sleep deprivation can lead to stress and have a negative impact on your mental health.

  • Developing coping skills. Coping skills can help you deal with your feelings in a healthy way. Healthy coping strategies may soothe you, temporarily distract you, or help you tolerate your distress.

  • Getting professional help if you need it.

  • You can use some of the resources provided below, you can call or visit their website.

  • Where you go for help will depend on the nature of the problem and/or symptoms and what best fits you. Often, the best place to start is by talking with someone you trust about your concerns, such as a family member, friend, clergy, healthcare provider, or other professionals. Having this social support is essential in healing from mental illness, and you will be able to ask them for referrals or recommendations for trusted mental health practitioners. Search for mental health resources in your area.

  • Many people find peer support a helpful tool that can help in their recovery. There are a variety of organizations that offer support groups for clients, their family members, and friends. Some support groups are peer led while others may be led by a mental health professional.
